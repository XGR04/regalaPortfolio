Who are my artist inspirations?

By Xandria Regala

Michelangelo

His contributions, particularly his awe-inspiring work on the Sistine Chapel ceiling, have long captivated admirers like myself. Despite his initial reluctance towards painting, Despite his initial reluctance towards painting, notably defending the supremacy of sculpting; Michelangelo's dedication to his craft transcended personal preferences, evident in the meticulous detail and profound emotion imbued in his sculptures and paintings alike. Standing as testaments to his artistic genius, inspiring generations with their beauty and complexity. His works have remained relevant for decades to come, inspiring us to be amazed at the limitless possibilities of the human mind and serving as a constant reminder that true brilliance has no boundaries.

Artemisia Gentileschi

Artemisia Gentileschi, the renowned Italian painter of the seventeenth century. My admiration for Renaissance masterpieces led me to discover her. Her paintings are particularly captivating, skillfully conveying raw emotions while harboring a dark and intriguing backstory. My exposure during art history had primarily been to male artists. However, Artemisia's brilliance has provided a refreshing perspective, possibly also because I am slightly biased. The recognition of her skills and the acknowledgment of a woman's contribution to the art world drew me towards her work.

I am essentially just drawn to the older techniques of classical painting, appreciating the meticulous and unhurried process that creates masterpieces. The allure of ethereal and occasionally intense chiaroscuro further deepens my admiration for the artistry involved.

In contrast to the more traditional styles, I've found myself less inclined towards modern art. Perhaps it's due to a lack of understanding or a challenge in grasping the "why" behind certain modern art styles. However, I remain open-minded and am willing to explore art beyond its physical elements, understanding the layers of meaning and intention in the works.
